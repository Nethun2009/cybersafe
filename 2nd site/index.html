What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. With the increasing reliance on technology, cyber threats have become more sophisticated, targeting individuals, businesses, and even governments. Cybersecurity includes various measures such as encryption, firewalls, and multi-factor authentication to ensure data integrity, confidentiality, and availability.

How Do Cyber Attacks Happen?

Cyber attacks happen when hackers exploit vulnerabilities in systems, networks, or human behavior. They use tactics like phishing, malware, ransomware, and brute-force attacks to steal data, disrupt operations, or gain unauthorized access. Weak passwords, outdated software, and social engineering make targets more vulnerable. Staying vigilant and securing systems is crucial. .
